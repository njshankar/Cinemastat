The information on a given webpage will look something like this.

Film = Film title
Epic = Epic index; a measure of general critical acclaim based on a weighted average of reviews from different sources. A score above 90 indicates near-universal acclaim, a score above 75 indicates critical acclaim, a score between 60 and 75 indicates lukewarm or polarising reception, a score between 50 and 60 indicates mediocore or highly polarising reception, anything below indicates negative reception.
Rev. = # of reviews tallied for Epic Index. Year = Release year of film (according to IMDB) Run = Runtime of film (according to IMDB) Genre = Genre of film (according to IMDB) Vio = Level of violence in film. 1 indicates little or none, 2 indicates moderate, 3 indicates extreme violent content. Sex = Level of sex/nudity in film. 1 indicates little or none, 2 indicates moderate, 3 indicates extreme sexual content. E = Ranking on Empire magazine's 2008 list of greatest films (if applicable) SS = Ranking on Sight & Sound's 2012 poll of greatest films (if applicable) VV = Ranking on Village Voice's poll of greatest 20th century films (if applicable) T = Ranking on TSPDT's aggregate of greatest films (if applicable) T21 = Ranking on TSPDT's aggregate of greatest 21st century films (if applicable) AFI = Ranking on AFI's 2008 listing of greatest American films (if applicable) ABP = Whether or not film won ('W') or was nominated ('N') for the Academy Award for Best Picture ABD = Whether or not film won ('W') or was nominated ('N') for the Academy Award for Best Director AW = # of awards won by the film (according to IMDB) AN = # of awards film was nominated for (according to IMDB) FA = Whether or not film won ('W') or was nominated ('N') for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Film B3 = Whether or not film won a major prize at Cannes, Berlin, or Venice film festival.
