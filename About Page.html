My name is JJ Shankar, a college student. I created this website a few years ago as a personal project to guide me in my film explorations. It's a simple compilation of facts about the filmographies of certain well-known filmmakers, where I've tried to find consensus on what their key works are, and convey this information in an intuitive way. I've found this project to be very helpful for myself, and I hope you will find it helpful too! Most of the photos on this website are from Wikimedia commons. For any questions, comments, feedback, or concerns, please email jjillinish@gmail.com.

Film = Film title
Epic = Epic index; a measure of general critical acclaim based on a weighted average of reviews from different sources. A score above 90 indicates near-universal acclaim, a score above 75 indicates critical acclaim, a score between 60 and 75 indicates lukewarm or polarising reception, a score between 50 and 60 indicates mediocore or highly polarising reception, anything below indicates negative reception.
Rev. = # of reviews tallied for Epic Index.
Year = Release year of film (according to IMDB)
Run = Runtime of film (according to IMDB)
Genre = Genre of film (according to IMDB)
Vio = Level of violence in film. 1 indicates little or none, 2 indicates moderate, 3 indicates extreme violent content.
Sex = Level of sex/nudity in film. 1 indicates little or none, 2 indicates moderate, 3 indicates extreme sexual content.
E = Ranking on Empire magazine's 2008 list of greatest films (if applicable)
SS = Ranking on Sight & Sound's 2012 poll of greatest films (if applicable)
VV = Ranking on Village Voice's poll of greatest 20th century films (if applicable)
T = Ranking on TSPDT's aggregate of greatest films (if applicable)
T21 = Ranking on TSPDT's aggregate of greatest 21st century films (if applicable)
AFI = Ranking on AFI's 2008 listing of greatest American films (if applicable)
ABP = Whether or not film won ('W') or was nominated ('N') for the Academy Award for Best Picture
ABD = Whether or not film won ('W') or was nominated ('N') for the Academy Award for Best Director
AW = # of awards won by the film (according to IMDB)
AN = # of awards film was nominated for (according to IMDB)
FA = Whether or not film won ('W') or was nominated ('N') for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Film
B3 = Whether or not film won a major prize at Cannes, Berlin, or Venice film festival.

A film highlighted in light green indicates a significant work in a director's filmography.
A film highlighted in dark green indicates a film in director's filmography that is widely considered a masterpiece.
A film highlighted in gold indicates a film in director's filmography that is widely considered one of the greatest (top 300 or so) of all time or a newer film that has is already considered among the greatest (top 500 or so) of all time, or a film that is widely considered one of the greatest to come out of its respective country.
